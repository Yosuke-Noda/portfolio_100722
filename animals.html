Animals

  • Elephant

    Elephants are the largest existing land animals. Three living species are currently recognised: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. They are the only surviving members of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea.

  • Giraffe

    The giraffe is a tall African hoofed mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant- hoofed herbivorous- on Earth. Traditionally, giraffes were thought to be one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.

  • Gazelle

    The gazelle is a small, slender antelope that typically has curved horns and a fawn-coloured coat with white underparts, found in open country in Africa and Asia.

  • Cheetah

    The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is a large cat native to Africa and central Iran. It is the fastest land animal, estimated to be capable of running at 80 to 128 km/h (50 to 80 mph) with the fastest reliably recorded speeds being 93 and 98 km/h (58 and 61 mph).

  • Tiger

    The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. An apex predator, it primarily preys on ungulates.

  • Buffalo

    The buffalo is a large sub-Saharan African bovine. The adult African buffalo's horns are its characteristic feature: they have fused bases, forming a continuous bone shield across the top of the head referred to as a "boss".

  • Zebra

    The zebra is an African equines with distinctive black-and-white striped coats.Zebras are primarily grazers and can subsist on lower-quality vegetation. They are preyed on mainly by lions and typically flee when threatened but also bite and kick.

  • Hyena

    Hyenas, or hyaenas are feliform carnivoran mammals of the family Hyaenidae. With only four extant species (each in its own genus), it is the fifth-smallest biological family in the Carnivora and one of the smallest in the class Mammalia.

  • Lion

    "The King of Animals" The lion has a muscular, broad-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. Also it is an apex and keystone predator; although some lions scavenge when opportunities occur and have been known to hunt humans, the species typically does not actively seek out and prey on humans.

  • Meerkat

    The meerkat is a small mongoose found in southern Africa. It is characterised by a broad head, large eyes, a pointed snout, long legs, a thin tapering tail, and a brindled coat pattern. The head-and-body length is around 24-35 cm, and the weight is typically between 0.62 and 0.97 kg.

  • Vervet Monkey

    The vervet monkey is an Old World monkey of the family Cercopithecidae native to Africa. The term "vervet" is also used to refer to all the members of the genus Chlorocebus. vervet monkeys serve as a nonhuman primate model for understanding genetic and social behaviors of humans.

  • Rhino

    Rhinoceroses are large, herbivorous mammals identified by their characteristic horned snouts. The word "rhinoceros" comes from the Greek "rhino" (nose) and "ceros" (horn). There are five species and 11 subspecies of rhino; some have two horns, while others have one.
